#definition #number-theory

#### Definition
For any $\alpha \in \mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{ d }]$ with $d\in \mathbb{Z}$, the norm of $\alpha$, $N(\alpha)$, is equal to
$$
N(\alpha) = a^2 -db^2.
$$
#### Note
For $d<0$, $N(\alpha)$ can only be positive.
